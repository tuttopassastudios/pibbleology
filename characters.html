Characters of Pibbleology

For the central figure's main article, see Pibble. For unverified figures, see footnote-only entities.

The cast of Pibbleology is small, recurrent, and mutually unimpressed. The figures listed below are those who appear at least three times across distinct fragments, excluding minor recurring figures and the broad category of "people who once saw Pibble from a window."

Character traits are derived from the archive in aggregate and should be understood as community consensus rather than the intention of any single contributor.

Pibble (by choice)

Pibble

The titular figure. Short, vaguely spherical, unbothered. See the main article for the full treatment. In the ensemble, Pibble functions less as a protagonist than as a punctuation mark: present at the end of most paragraphs and occasionally in the middle of one for emphasis. [1]

Larnice

Larnice is Pibble's self-appointed accountant. There is no evidence Pibble has income, expenses, accounts, or any concept of money, yet Larnice produces a quarterly statement on time, every time. The statements are formally addressed, neatly typeset, and on each occasion contain the single figure 0.00, followed by the handwritten phrase well done.

Larnice is widely regarded as the moral center of the archive on the strength of a single early fragment in which Larnice closes a window that nobody else had noticed was open. Larnice's surname, if any, is not recorded; attempts to invent one have been gently rejected.

Oboe of Threll

Oboe of Threll is a wandering musician from Threll-Across-Water. Oboe is never depicted with an oboe; the name predates the instrument, predates Oboe, and is, according to a long-running joke, the actual reason oboes exist. Oboe's musical instrument of choice is silence, deployed with precise timing.

Oboe is the figure most likely to provide a marginal note in any given fragment, often a single word: continue. Pibbleologists treat these notes as approximately as authoritative as the fragments they annotate.

The Vaster

The Vaster is the archive's principal antagonist, in the sense that the archive needed one and the Vaster was nearest. The Vaster's scale is famously inconsistent: in some fragments it spans counties; in others it can be put away in a drawer. Pibbleologists have long since stopped trying to reconcile the two.

The Vaster's defining habit is the act of misplacing itself — forgetting, mid-confrontation, what it had been upset about, and wandering off in mild embarrassment. This is widely regarded as Pibble's only consistent victory condition, and as a moral.

Pibblet

Pibblet is variously described as Pibble's younger sibling, Pibble's ward, Pibble's echo, Pibble's reflection, Pibble's mistake, and that one. No two contributors agree on Pibblet's attributes, including whether Pibblet is present in any given fragment.

The standing community position — itself contested — is that Pibblet is real on Thursdays. The Pibblet question is the longest open dispute in Pibbleology and is treated with the affectionate exhaustion of a long-running family argument. See Canon disputes.

Aunt Margery

Aunt Margery is, on the available evidence, neither an aunt nor named Margery. The figure is included in the archive on the strength of a single, undated letter to the editor of The Pibbleologist Quarterly, signed your Aunt Margery, in which Aunt Margery offers extensive opinions on Pibble's posture.

Subsequent contributors have written further Aunt Margery letters, none of them by the original Aunt Margery, all of them now considered canonical. This is widely held up as an example of the archive's relaxed standards for inheritance of authorship.

Professor Quoth

Professor Quoth is the archive's resident scholar, meaning that Professor Quoth is the figure most willing to use the word therefore. Quoth's lectures — transcribed by an unspecified student — provide most of the archive's secondary analysis, including the much-cited Notes on the Down-Direction.

Quoth is depicted in marginalia with a long coat, a longer scarf, and a habit of being interrupted by Pibble at the precise moment a point is about to be made. Whether Pibble is in fact interrupting or merely already finishing the sentence is a matter for the reader.

Minor recurring figures

  • The Innkeeper of the Quiet County — never named, never raises voice, has views about cutlery.
  • The Index Boy — a contributor's name for the figure who silently re-alphabetizes any list left unattended.
  • Two Postmen — always referred to in pairs, never seen separately, suspected of being one postman seen twice.
  • The Caller — a voice on a telephone that has not yet rung. Pibble answers anyway.
  • The Hat — Pibble's hat, treated as a character on alternate Fridays.
