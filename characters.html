The Pibble Meme Family

Four neighboring memes share the same French bulldog subject and feed back into the broader Pibble cycle.

Handheld Pupper, the photo that started it all.

Handheld Pupper / Geeble

Handheld Pupper, also known as Geeble, refers to a photograph of a minuscule French bulldog puppy being held in one hand. Online, the image saw virality in early 2023, often being used as an exploitable for various memes.

The photo became the initial subject of Alienposting / Zeep Glorp memes that year, in which the puppy was crudely edited green and given antennas. The image also started the use of the term Pibble for French bulldog puppies. The original Reel was posted by Instagram user @813_frenchies_ on October 17, 2022.

T-Pose Puppy, a.k.a. "STOP FIGHTING!!"

T-Pose Puppy / STOP FIGHTING!!!

T-Pose Puppy, also known as STOP FIGHTING!!, refers to a series of image macro and exploitable memes featuring a small pot-bellied and cross-eyed French bulldog puppy — a Pibble — doing the T-Pose.

The image of the puppy being held up by one hand began making the rounds in May 2024, with captioned and edited versions of the image going viral on Twitter / X in July 2024. The meme is similar to Jotchua memes that feature meme edits of small puppies.

"I Am Pibble, Wash My Belly."

I Am Pibble, Wash My Belly

I Am Pibble, Wash My Belly, also known as I Am Pibble, Wash Ma Belly, refers to a viral TikTok audio of a cute, baby voice saying, I am Pibble, wash my belly.

The original sound was created in April 2025. Variants of the sound, which remixed it with different songs and audios, surfaced in the following months — such as Wash My Belly, Yay and Clean My Belly.

The 2025 Pibble Death Rumor.

Pibble Death / Pibble is Dead

Pibble Death and Pibble is Dead refer to an internet rumor that Pibble, the French bulldog puppy, passed away. The idea spread in viral memes and posts on Instagram Reels and TikTok in October 2025.

Pibble was not dead, though — Pibble broadly refers to any image of a Frenchie puppy. Also, the creator of the Pibble meme, @pibble12 on Instagram, confirmed that the dog in the original Pibble photo (the Handheld Pupper) did not die, per new photos and videos shared by the dog's owner on Instagram.
