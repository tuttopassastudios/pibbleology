Artifacts and Objects

For artifacts disputed on canon grounds, see Canon disputes.

The Artifacts of Pibbleology are small in number and large in significance — a property which contributors have long noted is shared with most of Pibble's belongings. Five items qualify as core artifacts; a larger second tier of minor objects is included for completeness.

The Second Spoon

The Second Spoon is an unremarkable kitchen spoon, of a kind found in many drawers, whose principal property is that it is the second. The First Spoon is never described — its existence is inferred from the Second's name — and the Third Spoon is generally agreed to be a different drawer's problem.

The loss of the Second Spoon initiates the Spoon Cycle of the Middle Era. Pibble does not pursue the spoon; Larnice does, dutifully, with footnotes. By the time the spoon is recovered it is generally agreed not to be the same spoon and to have been improved in spirit.[1]

The Brown Binder

The Brown Binder is a three-ring binder of indeterminate authorship containing the largest single body of secondary Pibble material outside the lectures of Professor Quoth. It is brown. It is a binder. Beyond this, all attributes are disputed.

The Brown Binder is the textual home of the minority Recent Pibble dating, which appears in a single footnote on page 47. Detractors point out that page 47 is, by the binder's own pagination, between pages 12 and 13.

The Listening Stone

The Listening Stone is a stone of approximately fist size, gray, smooth, and conspicuously attentive. It does not speak. It is, however, agreed by every figure in the archive to remember — a quality not otherwise demonstrable but consistently reported.

The Stone is said to be placed near any conversation in which Pibble participates, on the principle that someone ought to be paying attention. Pibble's regard for the Stone is, per the archive, polite.

The Pibble Hat

The Pibble Hat is a small hat, dark, round-brimmed, belonging to Pibble. It is depicted in approximately eighteen percent of all Pibble illustrations and is regarded — on alternate Fridays — as a character in its own right.

The Hat is famously the only Pibble-adjacent object known to own its own accessory: a smaller hat, sometimes called the Underhat, never depicted separately. Whether the Underhat itself owns a hat is a question that has been gently set aside.

The Fourth Key

The Fourth Key opens the third door. This is universally agreed within the archive. No third door is ever described, depicted, or named. The Fourth Key has nonetheless been carried, debated, and once lent, suggesting either remarkable trust in the eventual door or a community-wide indifference to its current whereabouts.
