About

What is a Pibble?

Pibble is an internet slang term for a French bulldog puppy, often pictured lying on its back with its belly up. The term pibble was originally used to mean pitbull in the Doggo-Speak trend from the late 2010s.

In 2023, the Handheld Pupper / Geeble photo and its exploitable series went viral online, showing a baby French bulldog held in someone's hand. The image, and others like it, were later called Pibble in image macro and viral video memes on Instagram and TikTok. Some image-caption memes from the time period nonsensically referred to the animal as Gmail.

An audio known as I Am Pibble, Wash My Belly also went viral on TikTok that year. The T-Pose Puppy is another notable Pibble image from meme history. In October 2025, a Pibble Death Rumor spread online.

Origin

From "Pitbull" to "French Bulldog Puppy"

An early pibble-as-pitbull image, 2014.

The original meaning (2008–late 2010s)

The original definition of Pibble was a Doggo-Speak term for pitbull. Its earliest known use on Twitter / X was a tweet from the account Hero the Pibble (@hero_the_pibble) on August 3rd, 2008. The term was used for pitbulls into the late 2010s — for instance, by the Facebook account Marvel the Super Hero Pibble as early as February 28th, 2014.

The earliest photo tagged with pibble in the Know Your Meme database was shared by user kkduer on March 14th, 2014. The first viral tweet to use the word was actor Patrick Stewart's @SirPatStew post on March 9th, 2017, captioning a video of his foster pibble Ginger — gaining over 14,000 likes in eight years.

The first Instagram post from @pibble12 (Aug 10, 2023).

The 2023 rebrand: Pibble as French bulldog puppy

Pibble as a name for a baby French bulldog didn't surface until 2023, coinciding with the rise of Instagram user @pibble12, who shared their first post on August 10th, 2023 (over 4,200 likes in two years).

That post showed the Handheld Pupper photo of a French bulldog puppy being held in someone's hand — first posted as a Reel from @813_frenchies_ on October 17th, 2022. Later on August 10th, @pibble12 shared a slideshow of Pibble images captioned Here is the pibble starting roster, cementing the new meaning.

Spread

How Pibble Took Over Instagram and TikTok

In the months after @pibble12's debut, the account continued sharing photos and memes of Pibbles — sourcing many videos from Instagram user @jmarcoz, a French bulldog breeder.

"Pibble eating fake Gmails" — @pibble12 Reel, June 2024.

June 2024 — first notable engagement

On June 2nd, 2024, @pibble12 first gained notable engagement with a Reel, sourced from @jmarcoz, showing two Pibbles playing. The text overlay referenced the Freaky and Papyrus Irony memes:

Pibble eating fake Gmails because Gmail is a white pibble with black spots and not freaky with papyrus font like grow up duden.

The video received over 56,200 likes in a year.

@jpibblemafia, "Gmail, you naughty pibble" (Aug 12, 2024). Watch on TikTok

August 2024 — the "Gmail" caption explodes

On August 12th, TikToker @jpibblemafia shared a Soft Irony video meme with a Papyrus-font text caption reading:

Gmail, you naughty pibble, come back.

Over one year, the video received roughly 252,600 likes, cementing Gmail as a common nonsensical caption for white Pibble images.

@leakyscrotum, "what kind of pibble is this?" (June 25, 2025). Watch on TikTok

June 2025 — niche-trend send-off

Pibble memes continued to surface heading into 2025. On June 25th, 2025, TikToker @leakyscrotum shared a video in the What Kind of X Is This? format, in which AI voices say:

What kind of Pibble is this? / This is Gmail.

The video gained over 220,500 likes in four months.

Related Memes

The Pibble Meme Family

Four neighboring memes share the same French bulldog subject and feed back into the broader Pibble cycle.

Handheld Pupper, the photo that started it all.

Handheld Pupper / Geeble

A photograph of a minuscule French bulldog puppy being held in one hand. Online, the image went viral in early 2023 and became a popular exploitable. It also became the initial subject of Alienposting / Zeep Glorp memes, in which the puppy is crudely edited green and given antennas (the Geeble). The image is what started the use of the term Pibble for French bulldog puppies.

T-Pose Puppy, a.k.a. "STOP FIGHTING!!"

T-Pose Puppy / STOP FIGHTING!!!

A series of image macros featuring a pot-bellied, cross-eyed French bulldog puppy — a Pibble — doing the T-Pose. The image of the puppy being held up by one hand began circulating in May 2024, with captioned and edited versions going viral on Twitter / X in July 2024. The meme is similar to Jotchua memes that feature edits of small puppies.

"I Am Pibble, Wash My Belly."

I Am Pibble, Wash My Belly

Also known as I Am Pibble, Wash Ma Belly — a viral TikTok audio of a cute, baby voice saying, I am Pibble, wash my belly. The original sound was created in April 2025. Variants remixing it with other songs and audios surfaced in the following months, such as Wash My Belly, Yay and Clean My Belly.

The 2025 Pibble Death Rumor.

Pibble Death / Pibble is Dead

An internet rumor that Pibble, the French bulldog puppy, had passed away. The idea spread in viral memes and posts on Instagram Reels and TikTok in October 2025. Pibble was not dead, though — Pibble broadly refers to any image of a Frenchie puppy. The creator of the Pibble meme, @pibble12 on Instagram, also confirmed that the dog in the original Handheld Pupper photo did not die, per new photos and videos shared by the dog's owner.
