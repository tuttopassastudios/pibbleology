Pibble

This article is about the central figure of Pibbleology. For the disputed sister-entity, see Pibblet. For unrelated cryptids, see Not a Pibble.

Pibble (/ˈpɪb.əl/; also rendered Pibbl, Pibble the First, or, in older fragments, The Round One) is the central figure of the body of fan-curated lore collectively known as Pibbleology.[1] Pibble is most commonly depicted as a short, vaguely spherical creature of indeterminate species, distinguished by an unblinking stare, an apparent immunity to embarrassment, and the habit of appearing in situations long after the situations have ended.

First documented in the so-called Scroll Zero posts of the early community archives, Pibble is considered by most scholars of Pibbleology to predate the archive itself — a position known among fans as the Always-Was Doctrine.[2] A loud minority hold the opposing Recent Pibble position, which is treated within the community with the same affection and dismissiveness usually reserved for one's least favorite uncle.

Although Pibble has no confirmed alignment, deeds attributed to the figure include: shutting a door that had not been opened, returning a borrowed item that nobody recalls lending, and, on at least one occasion, voting "abstain" in a vote that was not being held. Pibble's catchphrase, I have always been here, is widely attested but uniformly unverified.

Origins

The origin of Pibble is a matter of long-running and good-natured argument. The dominant Always-Was Doctrine holds that Pibble predates not only the archive but the act of archiving itself, and that any attempt to date Pibble is therefore a category error. Adherents typically point to Scroll Zero, a fragmentary document in which Pibble appears already mid-sentence, complaining about the temperature of a soup that no other character remembers ordering.[3]

The minority Recent Pibble position dates the figure to a specific, mundane afternoon and insists that all earlier references are retroactive insertions by enthusiasts. This view is supported by a single footnote in the Brown Binder and very little else, but its supporters remain undeterred, cheerful, and slightly smug.

Early lore

The earliest body of Pibble material — sometimes called the Pre-Index — consists largely of secondhand anecdotes, captioned doodles, and at least one disputed grocery receipt. [4] Despite the unevenness of the sources, three throughlines emerge: Pibble is small; Pibble is unbothered; Pibble has, in some hard-to-define sense, already eaten.

First sightings

The earliest contemporaneous sighting commonly cited is the Porch Incident, in which Pibble is reported to have been sitting on a porch that, by every other account, did not yet exist. The porch in question was subsequently constructed, by coincidence or by some quieter force, three weeks later. Whether this is evidence of foresight or of the porch's relaxed attitude toward causality remains contested.

The First Rule

Pibbleologists frequently refer to the First Rule, an informal community guideline phrased: If Pibble is in the room, the room is now about Pibble. The rule is treated as descriptive rather than prescriptive — that is, it is not so much obeyed as observed happening.

Important characters

The following figures recur across the bulk of the archive. For full profiles, see the dedicated Characters article.

  • Larnice — Pibble's long-suffering accountant, despite no evidence Pibble has finances.
  • Oboe of Threll — wandering musician; never seen with an oboe.
  • The Vaster — antagonist of indistinct scale; possibly a feeling.
  • Pibblet — disputed sibling, ward, or echo of Pibble.
  • Aunt Margery — not Pibble's aunt; not named Margery; included anyway.

Places and regions

The geography of Pibbleology is famously inconsistent, in part because no two contributors have ever agreed on which direction down runs in the archive maps.[5]

Threll-Across-Water

A small inland town distinguished by the fact that no body of water is actually visible from it. The name predates this absence, leading to ongoing speculation about a previous, wetter Threll. Pibble is said to visit Threll often; Threll is said to be flattered.

The Quiet County

A region in which sound travels half as far as it ought to. Visitors report a faintly polite atmosphere and a strong urge to whisper, even indoors. Pibble is said to be a citizen, though Pibble has not voted.

The Forgotten Shelf

Less a place than a recurring condition: any high shelf which, when reached for, contains exactly one item the reacher had forgotten wanting. Treated by some as a minor character.

Objects and artifacts

Notable items in the archive, summarized; see Artifacts for the full catalog with provenance notes.

  • The Second Spoon — an ordinary spoon whose loss generated a full cycle of stories.
  • The Brown Binder — a binder of contested authority; possibly forged; possibly correct.
  • The Listening Stone — a stone that does not speak but appears to remember.
  • The Pibble Hat — a small hat. Belongs to Pibble. Has its own hat.
  • The Fourth Key — opens the third door. Universally agreed; no door is described.

Running jokes and memes

  • "I have always been here." — Pibble's signature line; deployed in any context that contains a door.
  • The Vaster Misplaces Itself. — A community shorthand for any moment in which a much larger problem quietly forgets what it was upset about.
  • Soup not ordered. — Used to indicate that someone is complaining about a situation they themselves authored.
  • Abstain. — Pibble's preferred political position. See Glossary: Abstain.
  • Pibblet exists / Pibblet does not exist. — A ritual exchange; cf. § Canon disputes.
  • Already eaten. — Pibble's reply to all food invitations, regardless of context, hour, or plausibility.

Terminology

A short written message that arrives one day before it was sent.

An informal canon tier reserved for material everyone agrees on and nobody wants to discuss further.

Canon disputes

Pibbleology recognizes three informal tiers of canon. They are not enforced; they are merely sighed over.

TierNameDescription
ICoreMaterial referenced in Scroll Zero or in the Brown Binder.
IIAdjacentMaterial accepted by long-running contributors but not present in core sources.
IIISpeculativeMaterial introduced by one contributor and gently humored by the rest.

The longest-standing dispute concerns Pibblet, whose existence has been argued for, against, and around without resolution. A standing community agreement holds that Pibblet is real on Thursdays; this agreement is itself disputed.

Note. Disputes within Pibbleology are conducted in the spirit of the archive: at length, with footnotes, and with biscuits. No contributor has ever been formally exiled, though several have walked out theatrically and returned without comment.

Trivia

  • The phrase "and Pibble was there" appears 117 times across the archive and is correct in 116 of them.
  • No surviving record describes the color of Pibble's eyes; all sketches simply leave them as small, decisive circles.
  • The archive's longest single sentence concerns Pibble's lunch and runs to 412 words. It does not specify what the lunch was.
  • Pibble is the only figure in the archive who has never been the subject of a poll, owing to a long-running tradition of Pibble abstaining from polls about Pibble.
  • The Pibble Hat is canonically considered a separate character on alternate Fridays.
